Stairmaster UI

Personas & Storyboarding

Sketch

This interface is used to provide an exercise for users that focuses on the lower body and serves as cardio. The components such as the foot pedals to get onto the machine and the grips on the side handle makes the machine easier to use.

My Observations

  • All users got on the machine in the same way: they used the foot pedals and the handle grips to get themselves on the machine
  • All users climbed to the top of the stairs before beginning the workout
  • If a user had a water bottle, they placed the water bottle in the provided container attached to the machine.
  • All users had a phone and placed it within the phone holder
  • Users also had their own headphones and music on their phone and they did not make use of the stairmaster’s bluetooth capabilities.
  • All users chose the guest option when shown the beginning screen
  • One user then chose the custom workout option and the other users just clicked on the go option
  • Some users adjusted the speed using the touchscreen arrows that corresponded to those options, while others used the physical button to their side to adjust the speed
  • No user had to use the emergency stop button
  • Some users made use of the handles to their side throughout their workout and others did not and kept their arms to their side

    • Interviews

    1. How often do you go to the Nelson?
      • One user reported going to the Nelson once a week, while other users reported going twice or three times a week
    2. How often do you use this machine?
      • All users reported using this machine every time they go to the Nelson
    3. Why do you use this machine?
      • One user reported using this machine to get in their cardio
      • Another called it a satisfying workout because it trains different parts of their body
      • Another user called it a way to get their body moving
    4. What is your first reaction when you begin using the machine? What do you first do?
      • Two users reported immediately knowing how to use the machine because of prior experience with the treadmill, which has a similar interface
      • Another user reported that when first using the machine she was confused by the many options and their meaning. There were also some icons that she didn’t know what they represented, such as a thermometer icon which she didn’t know controlled the air conditioning.
      • All users reported confusion when first using the machine because they didn’t know whether to click ‘guest’, ‘login’, or ‘register’ but they all ended up (and continue to) click ‘guest’
    5. How would you describe the process of using the machine?
      • All users describe the process of using the machine as first getting on the machine and then pressing the buttons on the touch screen
      • Some users, however, press the custom workout button and adjust the resistance and time according to their workout
      • Other users just press the ‘go’ button and adjust the speed and resistance as they begin their workout
    6. Do you find the machine easy to use and understand?
      • All users reported finding the machine easy to use and understand
    7. Do you find that the machine fulfills its purpose?
      • Most users say that the machine fulfills its purpose
      • One user said that since they have just begun regularly working out they do not yet see progress from the machine
    8. What challenges, if any, have you encountered when using the machine?
      • One user reported never encountering any challenges
      • Another user reported that the machine was hard to understand the first few times because of the various options on the touch screen
